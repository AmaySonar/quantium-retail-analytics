## The Recommendation (Pyramid tip — lead with the answer)

Four prioritised actions for the upcoming half-year category review:

1. **Off-locate Tyrrells and small packs** in discretionary space near 
   Mainstream Young Singles/Couples zones to capitalise on confirmed

2. **Roll out the new chip layout** to stores similar to Store 86 —

   chip sales ($485,000)
4. **Target Older Families** with volume promotions — highest revenue 
   segment ($156,864) with 4.6 shopping trips per year

## Supporting Evidence

### Who is driving sales?

Total chip category revenue for FY2018–19 was **$1,805,172** across 
246,739 transactions from 71,287 customers. Sales were stable 
throughout the year confirming chips as a habitual staple purchase 
rather than a seasonal one.

The three segments contributing most to total revenue are:

| Segment | Total Sales | Share of total |
|---|---|---|
| Older Families — Budget | $156,864 | 8.7% |
| Young Singles/Couples — Mainstream | $147,582 | 8.2% |
| Retirees — Mainstream | $145,169 | 8.0% |

Together these three segments account for approximately 
**25% of total category revenue.**

---

### What is driving their spend?

The high total sales for Mainstream Young Singles/Couples and 
Mainstream Retirees are primarily driven by **customer volume** — 
there are simply more of them — rather than high individual spend. 
Their average spend per customer ($18.64 and $22.83 respectively) 
is moderate compared to family segments who spend $32–$35 per 
customer annually.

Older Families drive revenue through a combination of **high 
purchase frequency** (4.62 trips per year) and **strong per-customer 
spend** ($34.02) — making them the most commercially robust 
segment in the category.

---

### Price sensitivity and impulse behaviour

Average unit prices across all segments cluster tightly between 
$3.66 and $4.07. However **Mainstream Young Singles/Couples pay 
the highest average unit price at $4.07** — notably above all other 
segments. Combined with their lower purchase frequency (2.46 trips 
per year), this pattern is strongly indicative of **impulse 
purchasing behaviour.**

Mainstream Young Singles/Couples are also **23% more likely to 
purchase Tyrrells chips** compared to the rest of the population — 
a premium-positioned, impulse-friendly brand that aligns directly 
with this segment's higher willingness to pay.

### Trial store results

The new chip layout was trialled in stores 77, 86 and 88 between 
February and April 2019. Control stores were selected using a 
combined Pearson correlation and magnitude distance scoring 
methodology across pre-trial monthly performance.

| Store | Control | Sales uplift | Customer uplift | Significant | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 77 | 233 | +27.8% | +25.2% | No (p=0.13) | Extend trial |
| 86 | 155 | +11.6% | +12.7% | **Yes (p=0.012)** | **Roll out** |
| 88 | 91 | +8.6% | +7.2% | No (p=0.24) | Re-trial |

**Key finding:** Across all three stores the new layout consistently 
drives **more customers into the category** — not more spend per 
visit. This confirms the layout works through improved visibility 
and category appeal at the point of entry.
